Common Geyser Problems & Fixes for Lusaka Homeowners

April 2026 6 min read Hot Water Tips

Nothing is more frustrating than stepping into a cold shower in the middle of a Lusaka winter. Geysers are one of the most hardworking appliances in your home, and in Zambia, they face specific challenges ranging from power fluctuations to hard water mineral build-up.

If your geyser is acting up, here are the three most common problems we encounter at House Plumbing Solutions and how we fix them.

1. No Hot Water (Element or Thermostat Failure)

This is the number one complaint. When your water remains ice cold despite the geyser being on, it usually points to a burnt-out heating element or a faulty thermostat.

  • The Cause: Power surges from the grid or simple wear and tear over many years.
  • The Fix: A professional plumber must test the electrical continuity of both parts. Replacing an element is a routine job but requires specialized tools and draining the tank partially.

2. Overheating and Leaking from the Pressure Valve

If your geyser is making a loud boiling sound or you notice constant dripping from the overflow pipe outside, your geyser is likely overheating.

  • The Cause: A faulty thermostat that doesn't switch off the heating element when the preset temperature is reached. This creates dangerous pressure inside the tank.
  • The Risk: If left unaddressed, this can lead to a burst geyser tank, which causes massive damage to your ceilings and furniture.

3. Discolored or Smelly Water

Notice rusty or brown water coming from your hot taps?

  • The Cause: Internal corrosion of the tank or a magnesium sacrificial anode that has completely dissolved. In Lusaka's hard water conditions, anodes should be checked every 2 years.
  • The Fix: Flashing the tank to remove sediment and replacing the anode can extend the life of your geyser by 5+ years.

Safety Warning: Why DIY Geyser Repair is Risky

Geyser repair involves high-voltage electricity and pressurized water. Attempting to fix a geyser yourself without the proper training can result in electrical shock or severe scalding from steam. Always call a certified plumber for geyser work.
