Zambian Home Maintenance: The Essential Plumbing Checklist

April 2026 Home Care Lusaka Guides

Maintenance is often ignored until something breaks. In Lusaka, the extreme shifts between the hot-dry season and the intense rainy season can put immense pressure on your home's plumbing and roofing infrastructure.

To avoid massive bills and household stress, we recommend following this essential Zambian plumbing checklist at least twice a year.

1. Inspect Your Roof & Gutters (Pre-Rainy Season)

Before the heavy rains start in November, it is critical to ensure your roofing is secure. Clogged gutters lead to water backing up into your ceiling, causing mold and structural rot.

  • What to do: Clear all debris, leaves, and dust from gutters. Check for loose steel or timber roofing elements that might leak.
  • Why it matters: Ceiling damage is one of the most expensive repairs in Lusaka homes.

2. Check for "Silent" Leaks

A small leak can waste thousands of liters of water and drive up your bills. Check your water meter when no taps are running. If it’s still spinning, you have a leak.

  • Common Culprits: Toilet flutter valves and underground pipes.
  • Tip: Putting a few drops of food coloring in your toilet tank can reveal a leak if the color seeps into the bowl without flushing.

3. Service Your Geyser Anode

As discussed in our previous guides, Lusaka's water can be quite hard. This minerals build-up inside your geyser. Replacing the sacrificial anode every 18-24 months can prevent the entire tank from rusting out.

4. Test Main Shut-off Valves

In an emergency, do you know how to turn off the water to your entire house? Ensure your main shut-off valve is accessible and isn't seized up from lack of use.
